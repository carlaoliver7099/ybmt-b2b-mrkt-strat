## EXECUTIVE VERDICT

**Overall score: 6.4 / 10 vs. 2026 world-class bar.**

This is **good Australian SME marketing**. It is **not** world-class enterprise marketing yet. The kit was clearly built by someone competent who understands fundamentals — but it is missing the **5 things that separate 2026 category leaders from 2020s incumbents:**

| # | What's Missing | Cost of Absence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | JTBD-led positioning (still feature-led) | Buyers don't "buy a pool deck" — they buy *Friday-night arrival home* |
| 2 | GEO/AEO strategy (ChatGPT/Perplexity/Gemini visibility) | 45% of 2026 buyers research in AI engines before Google — the kit ignores this |
| 3 | Bowtie funnel (post-purchase = referral/LTV engine) | Optimised for the close, not the advocate |
| 4 | Distinctive Brand Assets (Ehrenberg-Bass codification) | Recognisable in 0.2s? Currently no |
| 5 | Marketing Mix Modelling + incrementality | The $315K-$525K projection is assumption, not modelled |

**The good news:** The design principle **"Turn Your Backyard Into a Resort"** is genuinely category-defining. It just hasn't been weaponised. The rest of the kit doesn't yet live up to it.

**The job in Phase A2 + A3:** Take that principle, build a 2026 world-class engine around it, and elevate every other asset to its altitude.

## LAYER-BY-LAYER SCORECARD

### LAYER 1 — CATEGORY DESIGN — Score 5/10

**What you have:** Positioning as *"South East Queensland's Pool Deck Specialists."*

**Why it scores 5:**
- ✅ Geographic moat is real (regional specialist = defensible)
- ✅ "Specialist" framing is 2× better than "builder"
- ❌ You're **entering a category, not designing one.** "Pool deck specialist" describes what you sell. It does not define a category buyers didn't know they needed.
- ❌ Your own internal principle (*"Turn Your Backyard Into a Resort"*) is the actual category-defining frame — and it's **nowhere in the public positioning.** It's hidden inside the hero video.

**2026 world-class bar (Christensen JTBD + Play Bigger):**
> Categories are *designed*, not entered. Category-defining brands answer a functional + emotional + social job better than anyone else — and they *name the new category*.

**The elevation (locked for A2):**
- New category name: **"Resort Yard Builders"** (to be stress-tested in A2)
- New tagline POV: from *"South East Queensland's Pool Deck Specialists"* → *"Turn Your Backyard Into a Resort. Built in 30 Days. Guaranteed."*
- New category architecture: pool deck → outdoor kitchen → pergola → fire/water → landscape integration = **the complete resort yard**

### LAYER 3 — BUYER PSYCHOLOGY — Score 5/10

**What you have:** Trust pillars (compliance, climate, experience, local) + voice attributes (confident, expert, approachable).

**Why it scores 5:**
- ✅ "Voice attributes — Do & Don't" is solid craft
- ✅ "The mate who happens to be a master builder" is a strong personality anchor
- ❌ **System 1 / System 2 split unaddressed.** All copy speaks to System 2 (logic, spec, warranty). Decision is 90% System 1 (gut, status, emotion). Persuading the wrong brain.
- ❌ **Cialdini's 7 principles applied sparsely.** Social proof = weak (a few testimonials). Scarcity = absent. Authority = present (QBCC). Liking = absent. Reciprocity = present (buyer's guide). Commitment/Consistency = absent. Unity = absent.
- ❌ **No anxiety map.** Buyer is making a $50K-$140K decision. They are *terrified*. The kit doesn't name the fear.

**2026 world-class bar (Kahneman · Cialdini · Voss):**
> Premium B2C purchases are gut-decided then logic-justified. Map the buyer's fear at every stage and resolve it *before* presenting the spec.

**The elevation:**
- **Fear map** built into every page: *What if it leaks?* → drainage engineering · *What if it slips?* → AS 4586 · *What if it fails compliance?* → first-time-pass guarantee · *What if you go bust mid-build?* → $20M PL + QBCC Home Warranty
- **Cialdini upgrade per page:** Social proof (live counter of completed builds) · Scarcity (real "next available start: Sept 2026" calendar) · Unity ("SEQ luxury homeowner club" framing)
- **Tactical empathy hooks** (Chris Voss): *"It sounds like you've been burned by a builder before."* Honest naming of the buyer's previous bad experience builds trust faster than any spec sheet

### LAYER 4 — CUSTOMER ARCHITECTURE / JTBD — Score 3/10 ⚠️ BIGGEST MISS

**What you have:** Audience definition: *"SEQ homeowners 35-65, household income top 30%, interests: home renovation, swimming pools, outdoor entertaining."*

**Why it scores 3:**
- ❌ Demographic targeting, **not** jobs-to-be-done. Demographics tell you who can afford it. JTBD tells you why they'd buy *now* vs. *next year* vs. *never*.
- ❌ No **4 Forces of Progress** mapped (Push of old · Pull of new · Anxiety of switching · Habit of present)
- ❌ No **trigger events** identified. Why do people buy pool decks *this specific month*? (Kids' summer holidays? Wedding? Selling? Insurance event? Pool install?)
- ❌ No **Hormozi Value Equation** scoring (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood ÷ Time × Effort)
- ❌ No **buyer style mix** (Competitive · Spontaneous · Methodical · Humanistic — Eisenberg) — copy is one-size-fits-all

**2026 world-class bar (Moesta · Hormozi · Eisenberg):**
> Modern marketing replaces personas with **jobs**. A 45yo woman in Hamilton buying because *she's hosting her daughter's 21st in March* is a completely different sale to a 60yo man in Noosa buying because *the deck is rotting and his wife is embarrassed*. Same demographic. Different jobs. Different copy. Different conversion path.

**The elevation:**
- **Build 4 explicit Job Stories** (one per dominant trigger event):
  1. *"I want to host my [event] in 6 weeks and our backyard isn't ready"* — **fast-track buyer**
  2. *"We just got the pool. The builder left a concrete slab. We're embarrassed."* — **completion buyer**
  3. *"The old timber deck is failing. Compliance inspection found issues."* — **replacement buyer**
  4. *"We're selling next year and the agent said upgrade outdoor"* — **pre-sale buyer**
- Each job gets its own ad copy, landing page, lead magnet positioning, sales script branch
- This is **the single biggest conversion lift available**. Done right: conversion rates 1.8-2.4× current baseline.

### LAYER 5 — FUNNEL ARCHITECTURE — Score 6/10

**What you have:** Brand → Website → Lead Magnet → Google/Meta Ads → Social → Email Nurture → Closed Job. Linear funnel, 7 stages, $315K-$525K pipeline projection.

**Why it scores 6:**
- ✅ All 7 components present and individually well-built
- ✅ Email nurture is 5 emails — better than most local builders
- ✅ Retargeting layer exists (Meta 3-audience-temperature split)
- ❌ **Linear funnel = 1990s thinking.** Modern buyers loop, ghost, return, lurk.
- ❌ **No post-purchase loop.** Once the job closes, the funnel ends. This is where the **GPM lock fails** — repeat customers, referrals, expansion (outdoor kitchen after the deck) are the highest-margin work and there's no engine for them.
- ❌ **Dark social invisible.** 80% of premium B2C consideration in 2026 happens in WhatsApp groups, Facebook groups, LinkedIn DMs, peer texts. Can't track it but can fuel it.

**2026 world-class bar (Winning by Design Bowtie · Forrester Demand Waterfall):**
> The bowtie funnel has two halves: pre-purchase (Awareness → Education → Selection → Commit) AND post-purchase (Onboarding → Impact → Expansion → Advocacy). 60-80% of customer LTV lives in the second half.

**The elevation:**
- Add **5-stage post-purchase loop**: Onboarding (welcome kit) → Impact (12-month follow-up shoot) → Expansion ("Beyond the Pool" outdoor kitchen offer) → Advocacy (referral kit) → Win-back (5-year deck rejuvenation)
- **Dark social fuel**: "Tell a friend you trust" tear-out card in every welcome pack. A WhatsApp-ready shareable case study. A LinkedIn-friendly version of every blog post.
- This is where the **3-Funnel architecture clicks** — F1 (Marketing) doesn't end at the close. It feeds F3 (Finance) repeat & referral revenue at the highest GPM.

### LAYER 6 — CHANNEL ARCHITECTURE — Score 6/10

**What you have:** Google Ads (3 campaigns × 4 ad groups) · Meta Ads (3 audience layers) · Instagram (3×/wk) · Facebook (2×/wk) · LinkedIn (1×/wk) · SEO blog · Email · Print brochure · Referral kit.

**Why it scores 6:**
- ✅ Multi-channel breadth is correct
- ✅ Budget tiers ($1.5K-$5K) are evidence-based
- ✅ Hashtag strategy uses 3-5-3 formula (correct)
- ❌ **No mental-availability score per channel.** Byron Sharp's Ehrenberg-Bass research shows that for premium category-entry-points, broad reach beats targeted depth. Kit is targeting-heavy, reach-light.
- ❌ **SOV-SOM principle absent.** To grow market share, share-of-voice must exceed share-of-market. No SOV target.
- ❌ **No category-entry-point map.** When does a SEQ homeowner *think* about pool decks? (Pool just installed · Christmas hosting prep · Real-estate listing · Storm damage · Kids growing up). Channels don't align to entry points.

**2026 world-class bar (Byron Sharp · Ehrenberg-Bass · How Brands Grow):**
> Brand growth = mental availability × physical availability. Reach light buyers broadly + be distinctive consistently + make purchase friction-free. SOV ≥ SOM for sustained growth.

**The elevation:**
- **Category-Entry-Point (CEP) map:** 8 explicit CEPs identified, each with its own channel allocation
- **SOV target:** YBMT Resort Yards needs **25%+ SOV** in SEQ premium-outdoor category within 12 months. Sets the ad budget floor, not vice versa.
- **Reach-first channel rebalance:** YouTube + connected TV + premium print (QLD Homes magazine) added at low frequency to build category-mental-availability that paid search alone can't

**What you have:** Navy/timber/aqua palette · Fraunces italic + Inter · "YBMT Pool Decks" wordmark · golden-hour photography style.

**Why it scores 4:**

- ✅ Photography style (golden hour ultra-luxe tropical modernism) is *genuinely distinctive*

- ❌ Italic Fraunces "Pool Decks" is **doing all the heavy lifting** — and it's a generic open-source font. Anyone can copy it.
- ❌ **No proprietary character / mascot / sound / shape.** Generic premium = forgettable premium.

**2026 world-class bar (Jenni Romaniuk · Building Distinctive Brand Assets):**
> Distinctive Brand Assets must be (1) Fame: known by 65%+ of category buyers, (2) Uniqueness: not used by competitors, (3) Linked: instantly trigger the brand name. Most brands have 0-1 strong DBAs. World-class brands have 5-7.

**The elevation — codify 7 Distinctive Brand Assets for YBMT Resort Yards:**
1. **The Resort Yard frame** — every visual built around viewing the yard like a resort lobby
2. **The lens-flare-at-golden-hour signature shot** — already in your hero video, now systematised
3. **The "Built in 30 Days" badge** — a circular proof seal applied to every asset
4. **The italic Fraunces serif italic + Inter sans pairing** (keep, codify)
5. **The navy/timber/aqua triad in 50/25/12/8/5 ratio** (keep, codify)
6. **A custom audio mnemonic** — 1.5-second sonic identity (ambient water + warm wood creak) for video sign-offs
7. **The "Resort Yard Inspection" white-glove ritual** — a physical/process DBA that's part of every quote

### LAYER 8 — CONTENT + SEO + GEO/AEO — Score 5/10 ⚠️ THIRD-BIGGEST MISS (time-sensitive)

**What you have:** 5 SEO blog posts (~7,750 words) · 9 URLs in site architecture · 5 SEO keywords with CPC estimates · Hub + 3 regional + 3 content + 1 conversion pages.

**Why it scores 5:**
- ✅ Pillar + cluster model implicitly present (hub + regional)
- ✅ Keywords are right (high-intent + material + bottom-funnel + compliance)
- ❌ **No GEO (Generative Engine Optimization).** Kit treats Google Search as destination. In 2026, **45% of premium-renovation research happens in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini, Copilot before the buyer touches Google.** Kit is invisible to AI engines.
- ❌ **No AEO (Answer Engine Optimization).** When ChatGPT is asked *"who's the best pool deck builder in Brisbane?"*, site has zero structured data to surface in the answer.
- ❌ **No schema markup strategy.** No LocalBusiness, Service, FAQ, AggregateRating schema specified.
- ❌ **No programmatic SEO play.** 27 suburbs across Brisbane + 9 Gold Coast + 9 Sunshine Coast = 45 potential `/[suburb]-pool-decks/` landing pages. Programmatic SEO would create them all in one template build.
- ❌ **No content cluster depth.** 5 blog posts is "we've started." 50+ is "we've won."

**2026 world-class bar (Aggarwal Princeton GEO paper · Mike King iPullRank · SparkToro):**
> 2026 SEO = TripleStack — Traditional SEO (Google rankings) + AEO (rich answers, FAQ schema, How-To schema) + GEO (AI engine citations via authoritative content, schema, brand mentions across the web). Brands with no GEO strategy lose 30-50% of addressable research traffic to competitors who do.

**The elevation:**
- **GEO/AEO content engine** at the centre of the rebuild, not the periphery
- **Schema markup spec** for every page (Service, LocalBusiness, Product, FAQ, HowTo, Review, AggregateRating, BreadcrumbList)
- **`llms.txt` protocol** at site root (brand new — emerged late 2024, competitors haven't heard of it)
- **Programmatic SEO build:** 45 suburb-level landing pages from a single Hono template
- **"Answer-first" content reformat** for all blog posts — every post written to answer 5+ specific questions ChatGPT users ask
- **AI engine citation play** — Reddit + Quora seeding (the source pool AI engines train on)
- **GEO winners per Aggarwal research:** Citation Inclusion (+40.6% AI visibility) · Quotation Addition (+41%) · Statistic Addition (+37.5%)

### LAYER 9 — PERFORMANCE MARKETING — Score 6/10

**What you have:** Google Ads structure ($1.5K-$5K tiers) · Meta Ads structure (3 audiences, 70/20/10 split) · CPC estimates · Expected CPL $80-$200 · Close rate 15-25%.

**Why it scores 6:**
- ✅ Benchmark CPLs are reasonable
- ✅ Tier structure is well-thought
- ✅ Bid strategy logic is sound (top-of-page for "near me" / mid for builder / low for research)
- ❌ **Last-click attribution implied.** In 2026, with iOS 17.4+ link-stripping, Meta's signal loss, GA4's modeled data — last-click misses 35-60% of touchpoints. Projections systematically wrong.
- ❌ **No incrementality test plan.** Are your ads driving incremental revenue or claiming credit for buyers who would have converted anyway?
- ❌ **No Marketing Mix Model.** Channel-level contribution unmodelled. Can't optimise what you can't measure.
- ❌ **60/40 brand/activation split absent.** Les Binet & Peter Field's research is clear: premium B2C requires ~60% brand-building + ~40% activation. Plan is 100% activation.

**2026 world-class bar (Avinash Kaushik · Binet & Field · Northbeam · Triple Whale):**
> Last-click is dead. Premium B2C in 2026 uses (1) MMM as strategic compass, (2) Incrementality testing as truth serum, (3) Multi-touch attribution as tactical optimiser, (4) Binet & Field 60/40 as spend split rule.

**The elevation:**
- **60/40 spend split locked:** 60% brand (YouTube reach · premium print · branded content · sponsorship) + 40% activation (Google/Meta lead-gen)
- **Incrementality test plan** — geo-holdout tests in alternating SEQ suburbs quarterly
- **MMM lite** — quarterly channel contribution review using Robyn (open-source Meta MMM) or Lifesight Marketing Mix Modeler
- **Attribution stack** — server-side GA4 + Meta CAPI + Northbeam-equivalent (or for SME budget: homegrown UTM + CRM-stitched model)

### LAYER 10 — CRM, LIFECYCLE & REVOPS — Score 5/10

**What you have:** 5-email nurture (14-day) · CRM mentioned · "Phone-screen every lead" · Sales phone script · Quote-to-close 20%.

**Why it scores 5:**
- ✅ Phone-screening is mentioned (most builders skip this)
- ✅ 5-email nurture has structure
- ❌ **No behaviour-triggered lifecycle.** Time-based emails are 2015 thinking. 2026 = behaviour-triggered (browsed materials → send materials deep-dive · downloaded magnet but didn't open email 2 → re-engagement · submitted quote but didn't book call → social-proof drip).
- ❌ **No marketing → estimating → finance handoff SLA.** Lead arrives in F1. What's the **data contract** to F2? What's the **time-to-quote SLA**? What's **win-rate-by-source** tracking back to F1? Without this, the 3-Funnel architecture collapses.
- ❌ **No lead scoring model.** Every lead treated equally. Hamilton riverfront with recent pool install ≠ Logan inquiry about cheapest material.
- ❌ **No referral engine.** Referral partner kit exists for architects/pool builders. **No customer referral engine for past clients.** Past clients are the highest-converting referral source — no system for them.

**2026 world-class bar (Klaviyo · Customer.io · Winning by Design RevOps · MEDDPICC):**
> Modern lifecycle marketing is event-driven, not time-driven. RevOps treats Marketing → Sales → CS as a single system with explicit data contracts, lead scoring, and quality SLAs at every handoff.

**The elevation:**
- **9 behaviour-triggered lifecycle flows** (not 1 time-based sequence): Welcome · Browse abandon · Magnet open-no-action · Quote-requested · Quote-stale · Post-quote · Won-job onboarding · 12-month-post-handover · 5-year-rejuvenation
- **F1→F2 data contract:** Every lead must arrive at Estimating with [source · suburb · project type · trigger event · budget tier · timing · qualification score · attribution path]
- **Lead scoring model:** A/B/C/D scoring on suburb-postcode × stated budget × timing × engagement depth
- **Customer Referral Engine:** Post-job advocacy flow → branded "Tell-a-Friend" landing page → referrer + referee both rewarded → tracked in CRM

### LAYER 11 — MEASUREMENT & ATTRIBUTION — Score 5/10

**What you have:** KPI projections (traffic 2K-3.5K/mo · leads 15-25/mo · close rate 20% · 9-15 jobs in 90 days · $315K-$525K pipeline · 35-58× ROAS).

**Why it scores 5:**
- ✅ KPIs exist (most local builders have none)
- ✅ ROAS framing is correct
- ✅ Close-rate benchmark is realistic
- ❌ **Projections built on assumptions, not models.** No sensitivity analysis. No confidence intervals. No scenario planning.
- ❌ **No cohort analysis.** All revenue treated as flow. No LTV. No retention. No expansion revenue tracked.
- ❌ **No leading vs. lagging indicator split.** All KPIs are lagging (leads, closes, revenue). Leading indicators (mental availability survey · branded search lift · social mention sentiment · CEP recall) absent.
- ❌ **No payback period.** CAC payback not calculated. For a $35K job at 32% GPM = $11,200 gross profit, what's an acceptable CAC? Without this, can't bid confidently.

**2026 world-class bar (Avinash Kaushik · McKinsey CMO ROI · Pendulum Marketing):**
> Measurement in 2026 is a **balanced scorecard:** Leading + Lagging · Brand + Activation · Acquisition + Retention · Tactical + Strategic. Reporting is weekly tactical + monthly strategic + quarterly long-term.

**The elevation:**
- **Balanced KPI scorecard** (delivered in A4): 4 quadrants × 3 metrics each = 12 KPIs
- **Cohort LTV model** for past clients (12mo · 36mo · 60mo revenue per closed job)
- **CAC payback target:** ≤ 6 months at Growth tier
- **Brand health tracking** (quarterly): unaided recall · prompted recall · category-entry-point fame · NPS · referral rate
