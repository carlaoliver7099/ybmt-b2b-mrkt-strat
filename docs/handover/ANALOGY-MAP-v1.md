## 1. The Railway

**The strategy is a railway, not a roadmap.**

A roadmap is a flexible direction-setter — you can take detours, you can choose alternate routes. A railway is fixed track. You ride it in the direction it's laid, at the speed the engineering supports.

The 5 Congruency Doctrine anchors (Persona · Category · Promise · Economics · KPI) are the rails. You can't operate on three of them. You either lay down all five, or the train derails.

YDT's job is to drive the train well — fast where the track allows, careful through the curves, on schedule, with first-class hospitality for Sarah & Mark in every carriage. YDT's job is **not** to re-lay the track.

## 2. The Two-Headed Buyer (Units only)

**A Units deal is a 2-key safe.**

Units renovations need both keys turned at the same time:
- **Sarah & Mark's key** — the lot-owner. Pays the bill. Cares about kitchen finishes, disruption window, the disappointment if it goes wrong.
- **BCM-coordinator's key** — the institutional gate. Cares about compliance, insurance, the bylaws, the 27 other lot-owners who will complain about noise.

A traditional sales motion turns one key (Sarah & Mark's) and assumes the other will follow. It doesn't. The deal stalls at Stage 6 for 4-6 months. Or it closes, then collapses in week 3 of construction when the BCM-coordinator finds the bylaws-issue nobody surfaced.

MEDDPICC dual-scoring (≥12/21 on **both** sides) is how we turn both keys, in sequence, before we open the safe.

## 3. The Promise as a Whispered Word

**"Built like it's our own home" is whispered, never shouted.**

When a brand-promise is shouted (banner stands, pop-up ads, every email subject line), it dies. It becomes wallpaper. The audience tunes it out within 90 days.

When a promise is whispered — surfaced in exactly the right moment, on exactly the right surface — it gets remembered for years.

That's why the Units P-11 §11I sanctions only **2 physical surfaces** for the promise text:
1. The engraved Tasmanian-oak chopping board in the build-completion gift kit
2. The handwritten A6 card in the leave-behind folio (signed in Aqua ink)

Everywhere else, the promise is honoured by *execution*, not by *repetition*. The 7am-3pm site doctrine is the promise. The 14-day BCM Approval Sprint is the promise. The handwritten thank-you note 48 hours after a trade-show is the promise. The promise is what we do, not what we say.

## 4. The Bowtie (Cross-LOB Lifecycle)

**Sarah & Mark are one household, not three customers.**

The industry treats each LOB as a separate buyer-journey: termite person, pools person, units person. Three CACs, three nurture sequences, three retention strategies, three sets of attribution.

The YBMT thesis is the opposite. The same household hires us for termite at Y1 (their 30s — first home). They return for pools at Y4 (their 40s — second home). They engage us for units at Y12 (their 50s-60s — investment property or downsize).

The bowtie engineers this lifecycle in. Robyn MMM credits 50% of the pools-conversion-value *back* to the termite-CAC. The arithmetic only works because we accept that we're underwriting a 30-year household relationship — not a 30-day campaign.

When you see "30× LTV" on a board readout, the bowtie is what's behind that number. Without the bowtie, the number is 8-12×. With it, 30×. That's the whole game.

## 5. The Quality Gate as Airline Cockpit Check

**The Quality Gate is a pre-takeoff checklist, not a suggestion.**

Airlines don't take off when 92% of the cockpit-check is complete. They take off when 100% is complete. Why? Because the 8% you skipped is statistically where the catastrophic failure lives.

Same here. A pillar advances when 100% of the checklist clears. A forbidden state breach is not "noted and acknowledged" — it's "pillar paused, remediation chaired, then advance."

The 7 critical forbidden states in Units §12G are the engines on the wing. Any one of them failing pre-takeoff = ground the plane. Carla CFO holds the cockpit door key.

## 6. The Robyn MMM as a Doctor's Stethoscope

**Robyn isn't a calculator. It's a stethoscope.**

A calculator gives you exact answers to questions you can pose precisely. A stethoscope detects patterns you didn't know to ask about — irregular rhythms, distant murmurs, the carry-over effect of last quarter's spend on this quarter's conversions.

Multi-touch attribution (MTA) is a calculator. It tells you, with false precision, that this click drove this conversion. For Units (median 18-26 week F1→F2 lag, 38% offline touches), the calculator is **wrong by design**.

Robyn is the stethoscope. It listens to the whole system — decay curves, saturation curves, brand-equity-as-soft-variable, cross-LOB bowtie carry-over. It says "this channel is healthier than it looks" or "that channel is saturating, ease back."

YDT Data Scientist holds the stethoscope. Carla CFO is the consulting physician. The patient is the YBMT marketing engine.

## 7. Pureprofile as the Brand's Annual Physical

**Pureprofile is the annual physical — Robyn is the daily Fitbit.**

Robyn watches revenue every day. Pureprofile watches **brand-equity** every quarter. Both are necessary. Neither replaces the other.

A patient can have great-looking daily Fitbit data (steps, heart-rate, sleep) and still have an undiagnosed condition the annual physical reveals (cholesterol, thyroid, blood-pressure trends).

Same for brand. Revenue-attribution can look healthy month-to-month while the promise-association score quietly drifts down 15% QoQ — and 18 months later, the revenue follows.

Pureprofile DBA Health Panel ($72k/year SE-QLD panel + $24k BCM-coordinator + $14k gift-kit sub-study) is the annual physical. Skip it at your peril. Carla CFO line-item-defends this budget every year.

## 8. YDT as the Headless Pilot

**YDT is the pilot, not the airline owner.**

The airline owner (YBMT Board · Carla CFO) sets the routes, the safety standards, the brand experience, the destination. The pilot (YDT) flies the plane within those parameters with great skill.

A pilot who decides mid-flight to skip a stop to save fuel — or paint the plane a new colour without owner permission — is fired. Doesn't matter how skilled they are.

That's what MSA clauses 4.7.1-4.7.5 codify. YDT may execute brilliantly inside the doctrine. YDT may **never** modify the doctrine unilaterally. The headless model works because the head — Carla CFO — and the body — YDT — both honour the seam.

## 9. The 90-Day Onboarding as Pilot Training

**Days 1-90 is Type-Rating, not Day-One Flying.**

A new commercial pilot doesn't walk onto a 737 and take off the same afternoon. They train, simulator, supervised flights, line-checks. Same for YDT joining the YBMT engine.

- Days 1-14 = ground school (read all 90k words, ratify doctrine)
- Days 15-30 = simulator (stack stand-up, no live spend)
- Days 31-60 = supervised line-flying (3 LOB pilot launches with Carla chairing weekly)
- Days 61-90 = line-check (252-checkpoint audit, ≥92% to release operational authority)

Skipping any phase = unqualified pilot. We've all seen what happens.

## 10. The Annual Doctrine Refresh as Surgical Update

**Doctrines evolve through versioned surgery, not casual edits.**

Every January, the Congruency Doctrine + Depth Standard + Quality Gate doctrines get formally reviewed. Versioned (v1.0 → v1.1 → v1.2). Old-version assets audited for drift. Remediation plans published.

This is **surgery**, not casual editing. A doctrine version-bump means every operating doc may need updating. Every brief may need re-issuing. Every YDT team member may need re-ratifying.

The reason the surgery is annual (not continuous) is the same reason airlines don't change cockpit-procedures mid-route. Stability and predictability are themselves a doctrine. YDT operates the v1.0 doctrine for 12 months without negotiation. Then we surgically improve it. Then we operate v1.1 for 12 months. Etc.
